SuperStore Giant Project

portfolio / Superstore

Superstore Giant Company

July 2023

Background:

Superstore Giant is a retail chain based across the United States that offers a variety of office supplies.

Goal:

Understand Superstore Giant products and customers for enhanced marketing strategies.

Analytics Business Task:

Discover and analyze what products, regions, categories, and customer segments Superstore Giant should target or avoid.

Limitations:

Gathering additional customer data, including demographic information such as gender and age, can significantly enhance the depth and accuracy of the analysis.

Results/Suggestions:

Based on the analysis, the Western region exhibits the highest number of orders, with individuals buying for personal purposes making up half of the customer segment, while corporations rank second in terms of the buyer segment. A marketing strategy derived from this information will involve initially partnering with businesses, offices and school districts in the Western region to strengthen customer loyalty and expand Superstore's reach. Subsequently, we will extend collaborations to select companies and districts in other regions. These partnerships may involve providing supplies or offering discounts on specific products and on specific periods, like back-to-school season.

Furthermore, considering that binders have the highest sales and paper ranks as the second-highest, it reveals a complementary relationship. Additionally, despite copiers having the highest profit margins while being the least ordered product, there exists a complementary relationship between copiers and paper. Introducing promotional offers where copiers are discounted with the purchase of papers or binders could potentially boost copier sales. Conversely, customers purchasing copiers may receive discounts on paper when buying at specified quantities.

Statistics


What is the number of monthly orders?


Observations:
  • Orders spike up in September, November, and December.
    • September is when schools start, so office supply orders will rise.
    • November and December are the holidays so orders will naturally rise.
  • Orders drop to its lowest in January and February
    • Spending in November and December was high and as a result, consumers will save money the following months to make up for the loss.


What is the frequency of orders for each product?


Observations:
  • Binders have the highest number of orders
    • Binders are commonly used in any work and school setting.
    • The number of paper ordered follow behind the number of binders ordered.
      • Paper will be sold in stacks, where a binder will need to hold only a percentage of those stacked papers.
  • Copiers have the lowest number of orders.
    • Machines like a copier are a bigger investment for consumers but it is not something that is bought often. They are bought based on its durability.


What is the frequency of orders for each product, profits, and discounts?


Observations:
  • Copiers have the least number of orders but earned the most profits.
    • Copiers are a long term appliance investment, where customers buy it once (or until a new copier is needed) for a high price.
  • Tables earn negative profits.
    • The negative profits may be due to a high manufacturing cost and low demand.
  • Binders have the highest amount of discounts with the highest amount of orders, but profits aren't significant.
    • The discounts may be affecting the amount of profits earned.
