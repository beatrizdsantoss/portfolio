Data Analytics Job Posting Trends Project

portfolio / Analytic Trends

U.S. Data Analytics Job Posting Trends

October 2023
Data Source

Background:

Data analysts are responsible for transforming raw data into actionable insights, helping organizations make optimal decisions and gain a competitive edge. With the growing demand for data-driven decision-making, the job market for data analysts has witnessed an expansion. This project seeks to delve into the dynamic landscape of data analyst job postings.

Goal:

Gain an understanding of the data analytic job field through current job postings and evaluate their potential impact on the future of this profession, and provide valuable insights for job seekers and those contemplating a career in data analytics.

Analytics Business Task:

Examine the frequency of job postings on Google, investigate key aspects such as salary ranges, work schedules, remote options, required skills, and if degrees are required.




Observations:

  • Job postings:
    • The highest number of job postings in a day was 279 postings in November 4, 2022. The lowest was 1 posting in November of 2022.
    • Average of 3,682 monthly postings.
    • The number of job postings seem to be stable over the year.
  • Salary:
    • Salaries range from entry-level to senior-level jobs.
  • Job Schedules:
    • Majority of recruiters are looking for full-time employees.
  • Remote Work:
    • The number of remote work posting has no significant change over the year, with 13.85k remote jobs.
    • Majority of data analytics are on-site job locations.
  • In-demand skills:
    • Individuals who possess knowledge of the top skills (SQL, Excel, Python, Power BI), will hold an advantage.
  • Degree mentions:
    • Almost half of job postings mention and/or are looking for a degree in candidates.

Limitations:

  • The analysis contains limited data for one year, November 2022 to October 2023.
  • Out of 31,281 entries, 2,102 had data containing yearly salaries. This limited number of data may affect the accuracy of the salary average. Additionally, the distribution between entry-level and senior-level jobs may also influence the results.
  • The dataset does not reference any jobs that included hybrid work. Soley, remote and non-remote jobs.
  • Calculating how many job postings mentioned a degree involves filtering out the word, "degree." The term "degree" may not imply any educational qualifications, but may allude to different connotations or contexts.

Results/Suggestions:

In the data industry, the demand for data analysts remains consistently high. This presents a promising opportunity for job seekers and individuals contemplating entry into the profession. There has been an average of 93 daily job postings since November 2022, offering competitive salaries with an average of $100,000. While full-time on-site positions are prevalent, the trend is shifting towards more opportunities for remote work.

Prioritizing the development of specific skills mentioned in the analysis, can significantly enhance candidates' qualifications in this profession. Key skills for prospective candidates include proficiency in SQL, Python, a solid grasp of Excel, and proficiency in other data visualization software. While a significant portion of job postings reference the requirement of a degree, it's crucial to recognize the value of showcasing practical experience and proficiency in technical skills such as coding and analyzing, acquired either through past projects or work. This provides an opportunity for individuals who may not possess a relevant degree or hold degrees outside the field of IT to compete for these positions.

In summary, candidates are encouraged to demonstrate a strong understanding of technical skills, including SQL, Python, Excel, and various visualization software, through projects.
